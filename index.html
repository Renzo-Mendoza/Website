Renzo M. Mendoza Rabanal


I am a BSc graduate in Mechatronics Engineering from the Pontifical Catholic University of Peru (PUCP), currently working as a Research Fellow for the Zebrafish Brain-Body Circuits laboratory at Harvard University. My specialization lies in Control Theory, Dynamical Systems and Computational Neuroscience. I aspire to contribute to research toward developing mathematical or computational representations that describe complex feedback system.

Underwater Image Acquisition System with Lighting Control

Renzo Mendoza, Daniel Menacho, Dario Huanca, Diego Arce.

Designed and implemented an underwater system with inertial navigation and lighting control to register images to reconstruct deep-sea environments. Developed a lighting control strategy for low-light environments merging proportional-integral control and histogram equalization achiving 60% reduction of energy consumption.

Crowded Environment Monitoring System

Renzo Mendoza†, Daniel Menacho†, Dario Huanca†, Eder Quispe.

Developed and tested a monitoring system using computer vision and IoT technologies to help prevent SARS-CoV-2 transmission. The system was designed to determine physical distance among people and detect possible infections by measuring body temperature. Project presented at the ECI - Summer 2021 in Peru.
